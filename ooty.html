Ooty (Udhagamandalam)

Ooty, also known as Udhagamandalam, is a stunning hill station about 85 km from Coimbatore. Known as the Queen of Hill Stations, Ooty is part of the Nilgiri Hills and is famous for its cool climate, tea plantations, and scenic landscapes. The journey from Coimbatore to Ooty via Coonoor and Mettupalayam offers breathtaking views of mist-covered hills, dense forests, waterfalls, and sprawling tea estates. Some of the top attractions in Ooty include: Ooty Lake – A man-made lake where visitors can enjoy boating, cycling, and horse riding along the scenic shoreline. Government Botanical Garden – Spread over 55 acres, this garden is home to thousands of rare plant species, including a 20-million-year-old fossil tree trunk. Doddabetta Peak – The highest peak in the Nilgiris at 2,637 meters above sea level, offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and forests. Nilgiri Mountain Railway – A UNESCO World Heritage toy train that offers a scenic ride through tunnels, bridges, and tea plantations, making it one of the most beautiful train journeys in India. Tea Museum & Factories – Ooty is famous for its tea plantations, and visitors can explore tea factories to learn about the tea-making process and taste freshly brewed Nilgiri tea. Ooty is also famous for its handmade chocolates, eucalyptus oil, and locally grown spices. Whether you're looking for adventure, relaxation, or scenic beauty, Ooty is the perfect weekend getaway from Coimbatore.
