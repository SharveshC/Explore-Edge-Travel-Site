Maruthamalai Murugan Temple

Visit the magnificent Murugan Temple in Maruthamalai, which is nestled in the breathtaking Western Ghats just 15 kilometres west of Coimbatore. This temple dedicated to Lord Murugan is an absolute must-see for any tourist, with its marvellous architecture set against a stunning scenic backdrop. Discover the Arulmigu Subramaniyaswamy Temple, which is located on a scenic hill in Maruthamalai and where you can soak in the area's ancient history and natural beauty. This temple, built in the 12th century, is one of many Murugan temples perched on hills throughout the region. You can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding hill range and immerse yourself in the peaceful atmosphere that has drawn visitors for centuries from the temple. Come discover Maruthamalai, a hidden gem in Tamil Nadu! There are many fables and myths associated with this temple. Discover the fascinating story of Pambatti Siddhar, a renowned snake doctor and saint who once roamed the lands where our beautiful temple now stands. Legend has it that he used to extract venom from dangerous snakes during his childhood to make medicine for snake bites. However, one day he was enlightened by a sage who revealed the true purpose of life - to identify and control our inner snakes through the power of yoga. After contemplating the teachings and meditating on Lord Murugan, the saint finally received a divine vision from the lord himself. Today, our temple proudly displays the ornate gold and silver-covered idol of Lord Murugan, and during Artha Jama Puja, the deity appears in the form of Dandapani wearing a dhoti. You can also visit the shrine of Pambatti Siddhar and witness the deity in the form of a serpent. The temple is open daily from 06.00 a.m. to 01.00 p.m. and from 04.00 p.m. to 07.00 p.m. Don't miss this chance to immerse yourself in the rich culture and history of this shrine.

Perur Pateeswarar Temple

Location: Perur, Coimbatore
Deity: Lord Shiva (Pateeswarar)
Built by: Karikala Chola (2nd Century AD)
One of the oldest and most revered temples in Coimbatore, Perur Pateeswarar Temple is a magnificent shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva. This temple is believed to have been built by the Chola dynasty over 1,500 years ago and later expanded by the Nayaks and Vijayanagar rulers. The temple is known for its exquisite stone carvings, particularly in the Kanaka Sabha (Golden Hall), which features breathtaking sculptures depicting Hindu mythology and celestial beings. The temple‚Äôs main deity, Pateeswarar, is a self-manifested (Swayambhu) Shiva Lingam, making it a highly sacred site for devotees. The temple also houses Goddess Pachainayaki, the consort of Lord Shiva. The intricate Dravidian-style architecture, detailed sculptures, and ancient inscriptions found in the temple narrate stories from history and epics like the Ramayana and Mahabharata. A major highlight of this temple is the Arudra Darshan festival, celebrated with grandeur, attracting thousands of devotees. The temple is also known for its Theerthavari festival, during which Lord Shiva‚Äôs idol is taken in a grand procession to the nearby river for a ceremonial bath.

Koniamman Temple

Location: Town Hall, Coimbatore
Deity: Goddess Koniamman (Parvati)
Built by: 13th Century (Kongu Cholas)
Koniamman Temple is one of the most famous and powerful temples dedicated to Goddess Koniamman, an incarnation of Goddess Parvati. The temple is believed to have been established during the rule of the Kongu Cholas and has been a major spiritual center for centuries. This temple is known for its grand gopuram, colorful sculptures, and intricately designed sanctum. The idol of Koniamman is depicted in a seated posture with a divine aura, believed to bless devotees with prosperity, health, and well-being. The most significant festival celebrated here is the "Panguni Uthiram," when thousands of devotees undertake a spiritual walk (padayatra) to the temple. The temple is also famous for fire-walking ceremonies, where devotees express their devotion by walking barefoot on burning embers. The temple is particularly crowded during Navaratri, when it is beautifully decorated with flowers and lights, and special poojas are performed. Many business owners and entrepreneurs visit this temple to seek blessings for financial success and growth.

Eachanari Vinayagar Temple, Coimbatore

Are you looking for a spiritual journey that will leave you feeling refreshed and cleansed? Look no further than Coimbatore's Eanchanari Vinayagar Temple! This enchanting temple has a distinct allure that draws devotees from all over the world. The temple, which is devoted to Lord Ganesha is constructed in the Dravidian architectural style, exudes a tranquil, meditative atmosphere. When the temple was built in 1500 AD, its 6-foot-tall and 3-foot-diameter Ganesh deity was positioned within. Legend has it that while transporting the idol from Madurai for installation, the cart axle broke and the idol got seated in eachanari, where the temple was later constructed. Ganesh Chaturthi is celebrated with much pomp and pageantry here. Take part in the sacred and awe-inspiring Homam Yajna, a traditional fire ritual dedicated to Lord Ganesha, in the early hours of the morning. As one of the most significant forms of offerings in Hindu temples, attending this ceremony showcases your devotion and offers a unique opportunity to witness a time-honored tradition. There are descriptions of the numerous mythical tales of Lord Ganesha that are displayed around the sanctum sanctorum. You can receive tokens to offer Archana and book for prasadam through the temple trust. You can also sit in the area surrounding the sanctum sanctorum to meditate or unwind for a while. The temple is located about 12 km from Coimbatore city on NH 209 and is open from 05:00 a.m. to 09:00 p.m. with no entry fee.

Kottai Eswaran Temple

Location: Ukkadam, Coimbatore
Deity: Lord Shiva (Eswaran)
Built by: 9th Century
Located in the heart of Coimbatore, Kottai Eswaran Temple is one of the oldest temples in the region. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this temple is historically significant as it was built during the reign of the Cholas. The temple is famous for its unique Linga representation, which is believed to have divine powers. The temple features classic Dravidian-style architecture with beautifully sculpted pillars, a massive entrance tower, and a sacred water tank. The temple is surrounded by a strong fort-like structure, which is why it is called "Kottai" (meaning "fort" in Tamil). The annual Maha Shivaratri festival is celebrated with great enthusiasm here, with devotees staying awake all night, chanting hymns, and performing rituals. Another special feature of this temple is the Navagraha Shrine, where devotees perform special poojas to reduce planetary doshas (astrological afflictions).

Anaikatti

Imagine unwinding in a picturesque village blessed with the beauty of all elements of nature like the gushing streams, jaw-dropping waterfalls, verdant hills and rich forest life. The village of Annaikatti, situated 30 meters away from Coimbatore offers you nothing less than a splendid and tranquil experience! Anaikatti, meaning a group of elephants, offers you to experience the natural beauty of the Western Ghats. The village is also home to the Siruvani river, which UNESCO has certified to have the second sweetest water in the world. You can embark on a night safari through Anaikatti forest where animals like elephants, mongooses and the Indian bison can be spotted. It is advisable to maintain silence during the trip as the sound may disturb the animals. Also, during the day, you can enjoy the beauty of rainforests. The silent valley national park in the village is one of the last explored rainforests in the country. It also has a mythological connection as it is believed that Pandavas during their exile had visited the forest and it is also known by the name Sairandhri Vanam, another name of Draupadi and the river nearby is called Kunthipuzha, named after Kunthi. Jeeps are available for hire to enjoy the rare flora and fauna of the place. If you want to enjoy the rustic charm of the countryside, do not miss visiting Mangarai, a hamlet at the foothills of Anaikatti. The small village is surrounded by coconut groves and brick kilns. The ‚Äòkaapi‚Äô or coffee in this place is special for its blend of sweet and spice by adding dry ginger and jaggery. People often have steamed legumes mixed with coconut and legumes with this coffee. The Anuvavi Subramanya Temple and The Lalithambikai Temple are two temples that are famous in Mangarai. The Arsha Vidya Gurukulam provides you insights into Vedas, Upanishads and other ancient texts.

Siruvani Waterfalls ‚Äì The Hidden Gem of Coimbatore

Siruvani Waterfalls, located about 35 km from Coimbatore, is one of the most beautiful natural attractions in Tamil Nadu. Nestled in the Western Ghats, it is renowned for its crystal-clear water and lush green surroundings. The waterfall is a part of the Siruvani Hills, and the water flows from the Siruvani River, which is also the source of drinking water for Coimbatore. Interestingly, Siruvani water is known to be one of the tastiest natural waters due to its high mineral content and unique sweetness. Scenic Beauty & Serenity The waterfall is surrounded by dense tropical forests and rolling hills, creating a peaceful and untouched environment. The water tumbles down from rocky formations, forming a small pool where visitors can take a refreshing dip. The entire area is rich in biodiversity, with rare species of flora and fauna. The misty air, chirping birds, and cool breeze make this place a perfect escape from city life. Best Time to Visit The ideal time to visit Siruvani Waterfalls is after the monsoon season (July to December) when the waterfall is at its fullest. During this time, the surrounding forests are lush green, and the water flow is strong. Visiting during summer is not recommended as the waterfall may dry up. Trekking & Adventure Reaching Siruvani Waterfalls requires a short trek through the forest, making it a great spot for adventure lovers. The trail is surrounded by thick vegetation, streams, and wildlife, offering a thrilling experience. The Tamil Nadu Forest Department controls access, and visitors need permission to enter the protected area. Nearby Attractions Siruvani Dam ‚Äì Located nearby, this dam supplies drinking water to Coimbatore and offers a stunning view of the surrounding hills and reservoirs. Silent Valley National Park ‚Äì A biodiversity hotspot home to elephants, tigers, and rare species of birds. Kovai Kutralam Falls ‚Äì Another beautiful waterfall located in the same region, known for its rocky cascades and refreshing water.
