Vaidehi Falls

Vaidehi Falls is one of Coimbatore’s hidden natural wonders, tucked away in the Boluvampatti forest range of the Western Ghats, approximately 35 km from the city. This waterfall is a true gem for nature lovers and adventure seekers, offering a serene and untouched environment far from the noise and pollution of urban life. The falls are within a protected forest reserve, meaning that visitors must obtain permission from the Tamil Nadu Forest Department to access them. The journey to Vaidehi Falls is an adventure in itself, as visitors must trek through dense forests filled with diverse flora and fauna. The trek, spanning a few kilometers, takes you through winding paths, towering trees, and the sounds of chirping birds, creating an immersive experience in nature. The waterfall itself cascades down from a considerable height into a natural pool of crystal-clear water, making it a perfect spot for a refreshing dip. However, swimming is restricted due to safety concerns and the presence of wildlife in the region, including elephants, leopards, and various species of birds. The best time to visit Vaidehi Falls is post-monsoon, from October to February, when the water flow is at its peak, and the surrounding greenery is vibrant. Since it is a protected area, human activity is minimal, preserving the purity of the environment. For those seeking solitude and a true connection with nature, Vaidehi Falls is an ideal destination that offers both adventure and tranquility.

Perumal Peak

Perumal Peak, located near Kodaikanal, is a breathtaking trekking destination and one of the highest peaks in the Western Ghats, standing at an elevation of 2,440 meters (8,005 feet). Situated about 60 km from Coimbatore, this peak offers an unparalleled view of the surrounding valleys, dense forests, and rolling hills, making it a favorite spot for trekkers, photographers, and nature lovers. The trek to Perumal Peak is considered moderate, making it suitable for both beginners and experienced hikers. The 8 km trek starts from Perumal Malai village and takes around 3-4 hours to reach the summit. The trail is well-marked but can be challenging due to its steep inclines and rocky terrain. However, the effort is well worth it as the summit provides a panoramic view of the Nilgiri Hills and the lush greenery of the Western Ghats. The climate is usually cool, with mist often covering the trails, adding to the mystical charm of the place. One of the best experiences here is witnessing the sunrise or sunset from the peak, as the sky turns into shades of orange, pink, and purple, creating a surreal landscape. The best time to visit Perumal Peak is between November and March, when the weather is pleasant and the visibility is clear. Since the trek falls under a reserved forest area, it is advised to carry sufficient water, food, and warm clothing. For those who love adventure, trekking, and spectacular mountain views, Perumal Peak is a must-visit destination near Coimbatore.

Topslip & Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

Topslip and Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary together form one of the most important eco-tourism and wildlife conservation areas in South India, located about 75 km from Coimbatore in the Anamalai Hills of the Western Ghats. Topslip is part of the Anamalai Tiger Reserve, while Parambikulam is a separate wildlife sanctuary under the Kerala Forest Department. These two regions, separated only by a state border, are often visited together by nature enthusiasts, wildlife photographers, and trekkers. Topslip, at an altitude of 800 meters, is a lush green hill station with thick forests, grasslands, and plantations, making it a perfect retreat for relaxation and wildlife spotting. The region is home to bengal tigers, Indian elephants, leopards, sloth bears, Indian gaur (bison), spotted deer, and more than 250 species of birds. Jeep safaris and elephant safaris are popular activities, offering a thrilling experience of spotting wild animals in their natural habitat. Just across the border, Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary is a well-preserved forest area with a rich biodiversity, including a large population of wild elephants and some of the oldest teak trees in the world, including the famous Kannimara Teak, believed to be over 450 years old. The sanctuary is also known for its dam and reservoir, which provide a scenic backdrop for boating and eco-tourism activities. Tourists can stay in forest lodges, treehouses, and eco-huts, offering a unique opportunity to experience the wilderness up close. The trekking trails here range from easy nature walks to challenging hikes, guided by experienced forest officials. The best time to visit Topslip and Parambikulam is from October to March, when the weather is cool, and wildlife sightings are more frequent. Whether you are looking for a peaceful nature retreat, a thrilling wildlife safari, or an educational eco-tourism experience, Topslip and Parambikulam offer something for every nature enthusiast.

Ukkadam Periyakulam Lake

Ukkadam Periyakulam Lake is one of Coimbatore’s largest and most scenic urban lakes, covering an area of about 1.29 square kilometers. Located in the heart of the city near Ukkadam, this lake has been transformed into a vibrant eco-tourism and leisure destination. Originally built for water storage and irrigation purposes, the lake has undergone extensive restoration and now serves as a hub for birdwatching, jogging, and recreational activities. The lake is home to several species of migratory birds, including painted storks, pelicans, cormorants, and egrets, making it an ideal spot for birdwatchers and photographers. The walking track around the lake offers a peaceful environment for morning and evening strolls, while dedicated seating areas provide visitors with a place to relax and enjoy the tranquil waters. In recent years, the Coimbatore Corporation has undertaken beautification projects, adding landscaped gardens, play areas, and boating facilities, further enhancing the lake’s appeal. The sunrise and sunset views over the lake are mesmerizing, with the water reflecting the golden hues of the sky, creating a picturesque setting. Another highlight is the floating island, created to improve the lake’s ecosystem and provide a habitat for birds. The best time to visit is during the winter months (November to February) when the climate is pleasant, and bird activity is at its peak. Ukkadam Periyakulam Lake is not just a scenic attraction but also a testament to Coimbatore’s efforts in urban conservation and sustainable development. It is a must-visit for those looking for a peaceful retreat within the city, away from the fast-paced urban life.

Thoonakkadavu Dam & Parambikulam Reservoir

Tucked away inside the Parambikulam Tiger Reserve, Thoonakkadavu Dam is a hidden paradise located about 90 km from Coimbatore. Surrounded by lush green forests and rolling hills, this offbeat destination offers breathtaking views, rich wildlife, and complete serenity. The dam, built across the Parambikulam River, is an engineering marvel, contributing to irrigation and hydroelectric projects while blending seamlessly with nature. What makes this place special is the tranquil boating experience on the Parambikulam Reservoir, where you can glide over still waters with scenic reflections of the dense forests. The area is home to elephants, tigers, Indian gaurs, leopards, spotted deer, and over 250 species of birds, making it a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers. Unlike the more popular Topslip, Thoonakkadavu remains less crowded, offering a peaceful retreat for nature lovers. The Kannimara Teak Tree, believed to be over 450 years old, is also located nearby, adding historical significance to this hidden gem. Visitors can opt for guided safaris, nature walks, or eco-tourism stays in forest lodges, making it an ideal place for an immersive wilderness experience. The best time to visit is from October to March, when the climate is pleasant and wildlife sightings are frequent. If you're looking for an offbeat adventure with scenic landscapes and rich biodiversity, Thoonakkadavu Dam is a must-visit.
